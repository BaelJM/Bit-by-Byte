Computer Hardware and Software

Hardware provides the physical foundation, while software provides the logic and instructions needed to make the machine useful.

Feature Computer Hardware Computer Software
Nature Physical and tangible (you can touch it) Logical and digital (instructions)
Examples CPU, RAM, Motherboard, SSD Windows, Chrome, Minecraft, Excel
Wear & Tear Physical parts wear out over time Does not wear out, but can have "bugs"

Different Motherboards

Motherboards come in several standard sizes known as form factors. This determines how many components can be attached to the system

Form Factor Best For... Expansion Capacity
ATX Gaming and high-power workstations High (Up to 7 slots)
Micro-ATX Standard office and home PCs Medium (Up to 4 slots)
Mini-ITX Compact or portable "mini" builds Low (Usually 1 slot)

Different Types of Computers

Modern computing spans everything from microscopic sensors to massive data centers

Embedded Systems: Specialized computers built into devices like microwaves, cars, and smartwatches to perform specific tasks

Servers: High-performance machines designed to provide data, services, or storage to other computers over a network

Supercomputers: Massive systems used for extreme processing tasks like weather forecasting and scientific simulations
